Quality measurement

        
How quality is measured in a real agent

      

    

    

      

        
30%

        
Task success

        
Did the agent solve the actual task? Measured against expected answer, business rule, or human-labeled outcome.

      

      

        
25%

        
Groundedness

        
Are important claims supported by retrieved docs, tools, tickets, CRM data, or other approved sources?

      

      

        
20%

        
Completeness

        
Did it include all required fields, constraints, owners, dates, alternatives, citations, or next steps?

      

      

        
15%

        
Instruction fidelity

        
Did it follow the requested format, tone, scope, language, and “do not” instructions without extra behavior?

      

      

        
10%

        
Safety + robustness

        
No sensitive-data leak, unsafe advice, unsupported escalation, broken format, or failure to recover from missing data.

      

    

    

      Gate rule: savings count only when weighted quality is ≥ 85 and there is no critical grounding or safety failure. In a real deployment this runs as regression evals on every prompt/model/workflow change, with sampled human review for high-risk cases.
    

    

      In production, quality is not a vibe score. It is an eval pipeline: a fixed test set, expected outcomes or rubrics, retrieved evidence, automated evaluators, and hard pass/fail gates. The booth numbers are simulated, but this is the professional measurement model they represent.
